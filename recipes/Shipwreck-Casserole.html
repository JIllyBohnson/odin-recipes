Shipwreck Casserole

Shipwreck Casserole is a hearty, one-pot comfort food classic that's perfect for busy weeknights. This layered casserole gets its name from the "everything but the kitchen sink" approach - layers of ground beef, rice, vegetables, and cheese all baked together. It's filling, flavorful, and requires minimal prep work!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 cup uncooked white rice
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. Spread uncooked rice evenly across the bottom of the baking dish.
  3. Layer the sliced onions over the rice.
  4. Crumble the raw ground beef over the onions, breaking it into small pieces.
  5. Add layers of celery, green pepper, and kidney beans on top of the beef.
  6. Pour diced tomatoes with their juice over the vegetables.
  7. Pour beef broth evenly over everything. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, and paprika.
  8. Cover tightly with aluminum foil and bake for 1 hour.
  9. Remove foil, sprinkle cheese on top, and bake uncovered for 15 more minutes until cheese is melted and bubbly.
  10. Let stand for 5 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
